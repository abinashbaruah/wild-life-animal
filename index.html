Bengal Tiger

The Bengal tiger is a population of the Panthera tigris tigris subspecies. It ranks among the biggest wild cats alive today.

Native to the Indian subcontinent, these majestic creatures are known for their striking orange coat with black stripes. Each tiger has a unique stripe pattern, much like a human fingerprint.

They are apex predators, primarily hunting large ungulates such as chital, sambar, and gaur. Despite their size, they are stealthy hunters, relying on camouflage and power to ambush prey.

Habitat: Tropical forests & Grasslands
Status: Endangered
Lifespan: 10-15 Years
